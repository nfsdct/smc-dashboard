📋 EXECUTIVE SUMMARY — MARCH 2026
💰 Media Performance
March performance was mixed across campaigns, impacted by partial budgets, shorter flighting, and a strategic shift due to the geopolitical situation, while maintaining strong efficiency overall. Staycation was the standout performer, significantly exceeding all KPIs following a shift to broader reach optimization. Search campaigns (ADC AE and Generic/Brand) delivered strong efficiency and CTR-driven traffic quality despite reduced scale.
🌐 Website Performance
March traffic fell sharply to 659.6K sessions (−96.1% MoM), as large-scale campaign activity wound down and the regional conflict suppressed travel intent. Google, Meta, and Snapchat remained the top acquisition sources, with the UAE becoming the dominant market. The travel advisory page recorded a 35% partner link click rate — far above the 12.7% average — reflecting strong user intent to seek travel guidance amid regional tensions.
🌟 Influencer Performance
March influencer activity focused on driving strong local advocacy, with 81 UAE-based influencers (including 20 Emiratis) generating 50M+ impressions through staycation-led content. Eid activations including Hussein Al Jassmi and Ahmed Saad concerts delivered solid engagement. Overall performance was impacted by the halt in international travel, limiting full KPI delivery despite positive local momentum.
🏷️ Brand Health
TripAdvisor searches for Abu Dhabi declined by 82% YOY and 67% vs 2022. In Q4 2025, all brand health metrics improved across T1 markets: awareness +0.2pp, familiarity +0.7pp, consideration +0.6pp, and intent +0.4pp. Kuwait leads on awareness and intent; KSA ranks highest on familiarity and consideration.
📰 PR Performance
Destination: Generated 2.6B+ reach and 3M+ EMV through Eid content, Chairman and museum storytelling, and #InAbuDhabi integration. Corporate: Delivered 906M reach and AED 1.6M EMV across 329 clippings, significantly outperforming last year. Coverage focused on stability, guest stay extensions, and Al Ain's hotel refurbishment.
📱 Social Performance
Destination: Stable performance driven by trend-led and authentic content; engagement rate (0.39% vs 0.99% plan) and sentiment under-delivered due to reduced posting. Corporate: 1.7M reach; Ramadan content drove strong sentiment. Calendar: 701K reach; event-led content performed well despite reduced activity and lower sentiment scores.

✅ SUCCESSES
Staycation Pivot Drove Standout Campaign Performance
When the regional situation curtailed international campaign activity, a swift strategic shift to staycation-led targeting delivered results that exceeded all KPIs. The move to broader reach optimization unlocked strong impressions, engagement and traffic, turning a constraint into a commercial opportunity.
Local Influencer Advocacy Generated Authentic Destination Storytelling
81 UAE-based influencers, including 20 Emiratis, activated to champion Abu Dhabi through staycation content, generating 50M+ impressions with strong organic reach. Eid activations featuring Hussain Al Jasmi and Ahmed Saad concerts drove 3.8M impressions across 48 publications.
PR Delivered Outsized Reach Despite a Quieter Period
Destination PR generated 2.6B+ reach and 5.7M EMV, while Corporate PR delivered 906M reach (+400% MoM). A focused, high-impact approach leveraging Eid content, Chairman storytelling, and #InAbuDhabi integration sustained strong international visibility.
🔁 CARRY FORWARD
Strengthening Social Consistency & Sentiment Recovery
Reduced posting volumes and the geopolitical context drove engagement and positive sentiment below plan across Destination, Calendar, and Corporate channels. Rebuilding a consistent content cadence with culturally resonant, experience-led storytelling will be critical to restoring engagement rates and improving sentiment scores heading into Q2.
Scaling International Influencer and Celebrity
Contracted celebrity deliverables and international influencer trips were halted mid-quarter. Future planning should build in contingency windows and prioritize rescheduling of high-impact programs, while continuing to invest in group trip formats and creator collectives proven effective at driving content volume and viral reach.
